Predicting European Champions League Participants

Project Scope

We Choose to analyze the 5 largest European Leagues in soccer: La Liga, Premier League, Serie A, Bundesliga, and Ligue 1. We extracted team-level statistics in these 5 leagues for every season going back to 2010-2011 from footystats.org. We used this data to try to understand which performance categories were the best predictors of Champions League qualification, and then used what we learned to try and predict which teams would qualify in the current season. After running multiple regression models and machine learning models with this data, we found that about 30 of the original 190 inputs in our data played a significant role in determining Champions League qualification.

Analysis Summary

Based on the 30 fields we identified, we ran our model on the YTD 2020-21 data for the same 5 leagues to predict which teams were most likely to qualify for the Champions League. We also analyzed the features as well as their relative importance to draw conclusions about what characteristics drove on-field success. Most of the features the model deemed important are offensive in nature Goals per Match, First Team to Score, Failed to Score, etc), leading us to conclude that having a good attack is more important to team success than having a good defense.
